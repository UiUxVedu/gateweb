GATE Mechanical (ME): A Complete Guide for Aspirants1. Overview and Eligibility

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) in Mechanical Engineering is one of the most high-stakes papers in India. It is primarily used for admission into M.Tech/PhD programs and for recruitment into prestigious "Maharatna" and "Navratna" PSUs. To appear, you must be in your 3rd year or 4th year of a B.E./B.Tech program or already hold a degree. There is no age limit, allowing candidates to attempt the exam at any stage of their career.

2. Exam Pattern and Marking Scheme

The exam is a 3-hour Computer Based Test (CBT) featuring 65 questions for 100 marks. The paper is balanced between theory and high-intensity numerical problems. It consists of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ), Multiple Select Questions (MSQ), and Numerical Answer Type (NAT) questions. While MCQs carry negative marking (1/3 for 1-mark and 2/3 for 2-mark questions), MSQs and NATs have zero negative marking, making them crucial for a high rank.

Career Opportunities and PSU Recruitment

Mechanical graduates have the widest range of PSU opportunities. Companies like IOCL, ONGC, NTPC, HPCL, and BHEL recruit hundreds of Mechanical Engineers every year based on GATE scores. A rank under AIR 500 almost guarantees a call from a top PSU. Furthermore, specialized M.Tech fields like Robotics, Thermal Design, and Industrial Engineering at IITs offer average placements exceeding ₹15-20 LPA in the private R&D sector.
